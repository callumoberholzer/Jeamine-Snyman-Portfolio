THE BRIEF

">

< p >

We were required to create a theme for Vega Durban's 2015 Showcase. We identified our secondary target market as industry professionals as they will always come to showcase to see the new talent. Our primary target market is the apathetic Vega student. Our aim is to get them excited about Showcase so that they'll come whether their work is being exhibited or not. We want to call out a unique quality in them to make them feel part of a unit.

< / p >

< / div >

< div class ="

CONCEPT & EXECUTION

">

< p >

The concept is 'Those who see things differently' and is about calling out the uniqueness in each designer. To bring this to life we used anaglyph 3D as the base of all our design elements. Anaglyph 3D is the old fashioned 'red and blue' 3D. All the visuals can be understood without 3D glasses, however, the use of 3D glasses truely brings the elements to life.

< / p >


< div class ="

CONCEPT & DIRECT MAILERS

">

< p >

Anaglyph 3D glasses will be sent to industry guests and third-year graduates. The envelope contains a dual message, clearly visible when looking through a specific coloured lens. Curiosity is piqued, as the purpose of the glasses is not yet fully explained.

< / p >


< div class ="

POSTERS

">

< p >

The posters challenge perspectives through the positioning of the text and images, which encourages the viewer to move their head to make more sense of it. The posters also have dual images, which stand alone to create an interesting visual, but can be revelaed to be two different images when looked at through different lenses.

< / p >


< / div >

< div class ="

AMBIENT

">

< p >

The ambient ads challenge students to engage with their space, encouraging them to look around and see things differently, as the tagline suggests. A vinyl wall sticker in the bathroom is only comprehensible when seen as a reflection in the mirror, and a three-layered banner placed in a skylight on campus is only made sense of when one stands underneath and looks up.

< / p >


< / div >
