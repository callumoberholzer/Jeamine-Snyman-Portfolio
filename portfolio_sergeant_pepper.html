THE BRIEF

">

< p >

The brief required the choosing of a South African brand and creating a competition for them. A competition website and new media was to be used to address a problem the brand was having, such as lack of awareness. My chosen brand is Sergeant Pepper Clothing Company.

< / p >

< p >

Sergeant Pepper Clothing Company is “a minimalist hard wearing menswear brand that lives by the notion that ‘simplicity is the ultimate sophistication’” (Sergeant Pepper Clothing Co. N.d.).

< / p >

< / div >

< div class ="

KEY ISSUES AND AIMS

">

< p >

Through research the following key issues were identified:
1. There is a large brand buy in with those who belonging to the Hipster culture but the brand is not well known by the more fashion conscious, everyday man.

2. There is little customer-brand interaction other than the purchase process. Sergeant Pepper tends to feel distant and unreachable from a consumer’s perspective.

< / p >



< p >

The aim of this competition is to, therefore, increase brand awareness with men outside of the immediate Hipster bracket. As well as to increase customer-brand involvement thus creating greater brand ownership in the customers.

< / p >

< div class ="

THE COMPETITION

">

< p >

The participant must find a manly, heroic or gentlemanly situation that they can get involved with. They will then upload an image of themselves doing the Sergeant Salute in this situation to Facebook, Twitter or Instagram. The social media post must include the competition tag #SgtSalute, tag the Sergeant Pepper brand in it and have a short comment explaining how the situation that they were in was manly, heroic or gentlemanly.

< / p >



< p >

The winner (final and monthly) will be chosen by Sergeant Pepper, removing the 'popularity' aspects, allowing the best photo to win. This resonates with those in the Hipster culture who are about quality, not popularity. The prizes

are Sergeant Pepper apparel. The use of monthly prizes maintains interest for the duration of the competition by keeping the customers continually engaged.

< / p >

< / div >

< div class ="

SERGEANT SALUTE

">

< p >

The use of ‘salute’ is found throughout Sergeant Pepper’s copy and even on their clothing. It was this deep seeded connection saluting had with the brand that inspired the use of a salute in my competition. Captain Morgan did a similar thing by asking people to do the ‘Captain Morgan Pose’.

< / p >



< p >

The salute needed to be uniquely Sergeant Pepper’s so whenever that specific salute is done there is a link in the audience’s mind solely to Sergeant Pepper.

< / p >



< p >

A stylistic trend attached to the Hipster movement is the growth of facial hair, specifically beards. Thus I made the salute as follows: the man must take up a manly stance. He can be standing, crouching, even sitting as long as he looks typically manly. One of his hands must hold his beard (whether he has one or not) and look as if he’s stopped mid stroke. His other hand is left free to help with the manly, heroic or gentlemanly act he’s showing himself doing. And thus the Sergeant Pepper salute is complete and uniquely Sergeant Pepper’s.

< / p >
